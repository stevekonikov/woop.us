As a child, Drakas was known for being quite an engaged mind. In fact, you might call him an an über-geek although he really isn't one. That aside, he does make great use of computers and the internet and is pretty good at cooking. You'll see him bragging about how good something he just made tastes... and he'll eat it along with some noob meat too.

Drakas' first proper game was SimCity, which wasted much of his childhood on. Soon enough he was introduced to Quake 2, but did not take much interest in it, later encountering the fun Wolfenstein 3D. But in the later years, he found himself playing Unreal Tournament, where he was getting pretty much owned: it has a long history! At that point in time he was hooked on FPS. He advanced to Cube/Sauerbraten and Warsow soon enough. He also enjoyed playing Warsow, known as "Quake on weed".

In the days of old Sauerbraten, his competition was against rock.n.rol, sdk, nitronix, t0taln00b and others, some of the most famous players of the old days. In October 2006, Drakas, t0taln00b and Flapdrol, got together a team of those who want simply to pwn, because the clan competition was not big enough! That was when the "n00b-clan" was born. In a week's time, however, we renamed ourselves to the Woop clan, as we are today.

Drakas has spent a lot of time in managing this clan, managing (Fragfests and AC World Cup) and participating in various competitions (ESL, AC Cups) as well as running the HI-SKILL ladder for AssaultCube.

dazza got his first computer at the age of 5 and learnt to game with Nibbles and Reader Rabbit. He learnt to frag on Doom, Heretic and later Unreal Tournament, before quitting gaming to concentrate on his studies (or something...). After discovering Open Source, he returned to gaming with GNU/Linux and Sauerbraten.

With over a decade of fps experience, dazza has fragging figured out. While playing, he discovered a group of elite gamers calling themselves w00p; now an active w00p member for over 6 months, dazza loves to pit his will against the best gamers Sauerbraten has to offer, or relax after a tough day by putting noobs in their place in the public servers.

After suffering for some months on high-ping overseas servers, dazza used his linux experience to set up the w00p:au sauerbraten servers for low-ping fragging in the oceanic region. w00p continues to attract players from around australia with the promise of low latency and tough competition.

In between working as a software developer and sleeping, Dazza spends his time fragging noobs and maintaining half of w00p's server collection. Expect to find him in-game waiting for talented new players to frag :)

Of course, he sucked and of course he was the worst player on the server in that mode, but that mode just completely overwhelmed him. And soon it was the only mode he ever wanted to play. Quickly he turned out to be one of the best players on the server and he managed to get to instaCTF's worldwide weekly Top 10 players' list every once in a while.

Then, a few years later he started to experience the GNU/Linux world and dropped gaming almost completely for over a year. Then he stumbled upon to a game named Sauerbraten from his friend's suggestion (you might know this "friend" as another w00pie, teppo) and noticed how similar it was to UT 2004 and then he was hooked again. Due to his solid insta-background, he was instantly one of the better players around there and gained immediately delight and disgust (and also some "omgwtfn00bhackercheater") from other players. He joined w00p around the same time that teppo and Majikal did also and from that point on, w00p's reign in the PSL games has been unstoppable. Lately he has become interested into Urban Terror and has been improving in it a lot (well, the starting level wasn't too good).

wut's computer life began in 1984 when his parents invested in a Tandy Color Computer 2. From this critical point on he was hooked. Spending as much time in front of a keyboard eventually he had very little time to waste his mighty CPU power on trivial little programs such as games. Then id Software released Quake, and his computing life once again hit a critical juncture. Why waste processor capacity on mind-numbing number crunching and practical problem solving? Especially while just a few clicks of his new Logitech Mouseman 96 could turn wut from an unassuming teenage nerd into a state of the art, digital, fragging machine with a taste for virtual blood. A decade and change later and he is still fragging away.

However in the mean time wut does dabble in that useless practical side of computing, having had run own managed hosting business for a while, as well as dabbling in a little web development himself. He has been an avid Linux/BSD advocate since the mid-90s when X was about as fun to work with as a brain hemmorage.
